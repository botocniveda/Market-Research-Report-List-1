<p><p>Cord blood banking offers two primary market types: public and private banking. Public banking involves donating umbilical cord blood to a public registry, where it becomes available for anyone in need of stem cell transplants, promoting community health and research. In contrast, private banking allows families to store their newborn's cord blood for exclusive use, typically for potential future medical treatments. Both options provide unique benefits, with public banking focusing on communal use and private banking emphasizing personal future healthcare needs.</p></p>
