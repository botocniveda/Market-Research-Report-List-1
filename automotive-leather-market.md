<p><p>The automotive leather market comprises two primary types: genuine leather and synthetic leather. Genuine leather, sourced from animal hides, is prized for its durability, luxury feel, and natural aesthetics, making it popular for high-end vehicles. Synthetic leather, often made from polyurethane or polyvinyl chloride, offers more cost-effective, lightweight, and environmentally friendly alternatives, mimicking the appearance of genuine leather. This material is increasingly favored for its versatility, ease of maintenance, and resistance to wear, appealing to a broader range of consumers.</p></p>
