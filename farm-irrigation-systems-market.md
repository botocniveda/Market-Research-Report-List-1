<p><p>Farm irrigation systems encompass various methods to deliver water to crops, each catering to different agricultural needs. Flood irrigation involves inundating fields, promoting water absorption but often wasting resources. Sprinkler irrigation mimics rainfall, distributing water evenly over crops, suited for diverse terrains. Drip irrigation delivers water directly to plant roots, minimizing evaporation and runoff, leading to efficient usage. Micro irrigation, similar to drip, provides precise water distribution across smaller areas, ideal for specialty crops. Each system varies in efficiency, cost, and application.</p></p>
