<p><p>The non-residential occupancy sensors market comprises various types designed to detect human presence for efficient energy management. Passive Infrared (PIR) sensors utilize infrared technology to sense changes in heat emitted by occupants. Ultrasonic sensors emit high-frequency sound waves to detect movement, even through obstacles. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine both PIR and ultrasonic technologies for enhanced accuracy and reliability. Other types may include microwave sensors and photoelectric sensors, catering to diverse environments and specific needs in commercial spaces.</p></p>
