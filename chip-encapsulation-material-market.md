<p><p>The chip encapsulation material market is segmented into epoxy-based and non-epoxy-based materials. Epoxy-based materials are widely used due to their excellent adhesion, thermal stability, and electrical insulation properties, making them suitable for various semiconductor applications. In contrast, non-epoxy-based materials, which include silicone and polyimide types, offer flexibility, high-temperature resistance, and lightweight characteristics, catering to specialized applications. Both segments serve critical roles in enhancing the reliability and performance of electronic devices through effective encapsulation solutions.</p></p>
